 Data Insights

  • On average, states with higher educational attainment also had a fewer adults who couldn't afford a needed doctor's visit in the past year, more binge drinking adults, and fewer disabled adults
  • Adults with higher educational attainment may have higher incomes, allowing them to visit doctors as needed and preventing the onset of disabilities
  • Adults who had bachelor's degrees or higher may have had prolonged exposure to college drinking culture, and therefore be more likely to binge-drinking tendencies
  • College-educated adults may also have more disposable income to spend on alcohol

 Data Sources

  • Educational attainment: The Census Bureau's 2014 American Community Survey 1-Year Estimates
    • Less than high school diploma: Proportion of adults without a high school diploma
    • High school diploma: Proportion of adults whose highest degree is a high school diploma
    • Bachelor's degree: Proportion of adults with a bachelor's degree or higher
  • Health outcomes: The CDC's 2014 Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) Prevalence Data
    • Can't afford needed doctor's visit: Proportion of adults who've needed to see a doctor but could not because of cost within the past 12 months
    • Binge drinkers: Proportion of adult males having five or more drinks on one occasion, and adult females having four or more drinks on one occasion
    • Disabled: Proportion of adults dults who are limited in any activities because of physical, mental, or emotional problems
